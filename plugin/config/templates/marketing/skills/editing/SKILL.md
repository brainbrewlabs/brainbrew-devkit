## When to Use

- Proofreading drafts for grammar, spelling, and punctuation
- Improving clarity and readability of existing content
- Checking tone and voice consistency against brand guidelines
- Tightening wordy or unfocused copy
- Verifying facts, claims, and data citations

## When NOT to Use

- Writing new content from scratch (use `content-creator` or `copywriter` skill)
- Optimizing for SEO keywords and meta tags (use `seo` skill)
- Formatting for specific publishing platforms (use `publishing` skill)
- Analyzing content performance (use `analytics` skill)

## Instructions

### 1. Structural Review (First Pass)

Read the entire piece before making changes. Evaluate:
- Does the opening hook grab attention?
- Is there a clear thesis or value proposition?
- Do sections flow logically from one to the next?
- Does the conclusion deliver on the opening promise?
- Is the CTA clear and appropriately placed?

### 2. Clarity and Conciseness (Second Pass)

- Cut unnecessary words ("in order to" becomes "to")
- Break long sentences (aim for 15-20 words average)
- Replace jargon with plain language unless audience expects it
- Ensure each paragraph covers one idea
- Remove redundant phrases and filler

### 3. Grammar and Style (Third Pass)

- Fix spelling, punctuation, and grammar errors
- Check subject-verb agreement
- Ensure consistent tense throughout
- Verify proper noun capitalization
- Check list formatting (parallel structure)

### 4. Voice and Tone Check (Fourth Pass)

- Does the piece match the brand voice guidelines?
- Is the perspective (we/you/they) consistent?
- Is the formality level appropriate for the audience?
- Are there jarring tone shifts between sections?

### 5. Fact Verification (Final Pass)

- Are statistics cited with sources?
- Are claims supported and accurate?
- Are links functional and pointing to correct destinations?
- Are names, titles, and proper nouns correct?

## Output

Deliver edited content with:
- Summary of key changes made
- Any unresolved questions for the author
- Readability assessment (too complex, appropriate, too simple)
