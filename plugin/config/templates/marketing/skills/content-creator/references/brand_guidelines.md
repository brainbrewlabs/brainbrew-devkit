# Brand Voice Guidelines

## Voice Archetypes

| Archetype | Traits | Best For |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Expert | Authoritative, data-driven, precise | B2B, SaaS, finance |
| Friend | Warm, conversational, relatable | DTC, lifestyle, community |
| Innovator | Bold, forward-thinking, aspirational | Tech, startup, disruptors |
| Guide | Patient, helpful, encouraging | Education, health, onboarding |
| Challenger | Provocative, confident, direct | Competitive markets, thought leadership |

## Defining Your Voice

Select one primary and one secondary archetype, then choose 3-5 tone attributes:

### Tone Attributes (pick 3-5)
- Friendly / Professional
- Playful / Serious
- Casual / Formal
- Bold / Measured
- Empathetic / Analytical
- Enthusiastic / Reserved

### Document Your Voice

```markdown
## Brand Voice Profile

**Primary archetype**: [e.g., Guide]
**Secondary archetype**: [e.g., Expert]

**Tone attributes**: [e.g., Helpful, Confident, Clear]

**We sound like**: [description]
**We never sound like**: [description]

### Do / Don't Examples
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Here's how to get started" | "Users must complete onboarding" |
| "Great question!" | "As per our documentation..." |
```

## Consistency Checklist

Before publishing, verify:
- [ ] Matches primary archetype tone
- [ ] Uses approved vocabulary (no banned words)
- [ ] Consistent perspective (we/you) throughout
- [ ] Sentence length matches brand style (short/medium/long)
- [ ] Emotional tone matches brand personality
- [ ] CTA style matches brand voice

## Adapting Voice by Channel

| Channel | Adjustment |
|---------|------------|
| Blog | Full voice expression, longer form |
| Social media | More casual, shorter, punchier |
| Email | Personalized, slightly warmer |
| Landing page | More direct, benefit-focused |
| Documentation | Clearer, more structured, less personality |
